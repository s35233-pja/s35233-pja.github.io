Project Overview

The short 3D cinematic project was developed as an experimental study of mechanical interaction and procedural motion, focusing on robotic systems engaged in a delicate manufacturing process. The core concept revolves around autonomous robotic arms collaboratively shaping a ceramic vase, combining precision engineering aesthetics with stylized cinematic storytelling. The goal was to balance realism with artistic interpretation, emphasizing rhythm, timing, and visual clarity in motion design.

From a technical perspective, the animation pipeline was built using standard industry workflows, including asset modeling, rigging, and procedural animation techniques. The robotic arms were rigged with hierarchical joint systems to ensure accurate inverse kinematics behavior, allowing for smooth and physically plausible movements during the sculpting process. Special attention was given to motion curves and keyframe interpolation to achieve a controlled yet organic mechanical feel.

I used Blender for all modelling, rigging, and rendering. Texturing was done in Substance Painter using a PBR metalness workflow. The final render was made with studio lighting to give the mech its dramatic silhouette.

Post-production focused on enhancing cinematic depth through color grading, motion blur, and subtle camera animation techniques. The final composition emphasizes storytelling through movement rather than dialogue, showcasing the transformation of raw material into a refined object. This project served as an exploration of procedural craftsmanship, robotic motion design, and visual narrative structure within a compact cinematic format.

The project allowed me to practice several aspects of the 3D workflow, including modeling, texturing, scene composition, and rendering. Through this challenge, I improved my ability to create stylized environments and learned how small visual details can contribute to the overall energy and storytelling of a scene.

Tools Used

  • Blender 4.1
  • Substance Painter
  • Cycles Renderer
  • Premiere Pro
  • PBR textures
