Project Overview

This project was created as part of the Kinetic Rush challenge. My main inspiration came from the visual style and atmosphere of endless runner games, especially the colorful and energetic world of Subway Surfers. I wanted to capture the feeling of speed, movement, and constant action while keeping the scene visually appealing and easy to read.

During the creation process, I focused on designing a dynamic environment that would immediately communicate motion and excitement. The composition was built around strong leading lines, vibrant colors, and recognizable urban elements often associated with subway and street culture. Special attention was given to lighting and camera placement to enhance the sense of momentum.

I used Blender for all modelling, rigging, and rendering. Texturing was done in Substance Painter using a PBR metalness workflow. The final render was made with HDRI map cinematic lighting and a subtle rim light to give the mech its dramatic silhouette.

One of the biggest challenges was keeping the polygon count low enough for real-time use while still having enough surface detail to look convincing in close-up shots. The final mesh sits at around ~85,000 triangles, which is reasonable for a hero character asset.

The project allowed me to practice several aspects of the 3D workflow, including modeling, texturing, scene composition, and rendering. Through this challenge, I improved my ability to create stylized environments and learned how small visual details can contribute to the overall energy and storytelling of a scene.

Tools Used

  • Blender 4.1
  • Substance Painter
  • Cycles Renderer
  • Photoshop
  • Marmoset Toolbag
